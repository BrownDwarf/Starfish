Fitting many lines

is tough.

near: Even some of the better fitting lines will still have ignificant correlation in the residuals.

far: Alternatively, there will likely be lines that have incorrect atomic constants.

Derive a likelihood function which accounts for covariances in the residuals. Then, parameterize this covariance using a Gaussian process covariance kernel.

We can design kernels which account for the broad-scale, globally covariant residuals as well as the local covariance

$$ p (\mathsf{D} | \mathsf{M} ) = \frac{1}{\left [ (2 \pi)^N \det(\mathsf{C}) \right ]^{1/2}} \exp \left (- \frac{1}{2} \mathsf{R}^T \mathsf{C}^{-1} \mathsf{R} \right ) $$
